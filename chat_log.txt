Q: Explain deadlock with a simple example.
A: Deadlock is a situation in concurrent computing where two or more processes are unable to proceed because each is waiting for the other to release a resource. Here's a simple example:

Imagine two processes, A and B, and two resources, R1 and R2. Both processes require both resources to complete their tasks.

1. Process A acquires resource R1.
2. Process B acquires resource R2.
3. Process A now needs resource R2 to continue, but it's held by process B.
4. Process B now needs resource R1 to continue, but it's held by process A.

Both processes are now stuck, waiting for resources that the other process holds. This is a deadlock.

To avoid deadlocks, you can use techniques like resource ordering (always acquire resources in the same order), timeouts (wait for a resource for a certain amount of time, then release it and move on), or deadlock detection (regularly check the system for deadlocks and resolve them when they occur).
